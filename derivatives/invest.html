About Derivatives


Derivatives


A derivative is a financial instrument whose return and price are based on one or more underlying assets. They are traded in two types of markets: 1. a standardized, authorized exchange traded market (e.g. futures), and 2. loosely regulated over-the-counter market (e.g. forward and swap). Derivative contracts can also be categorized into forward commitments or contingent claims. Under forward commitments (e.g. forward, futures and swap), two parties agree to engage in a transaction at a later date at a price determined in the beginning . In contrast, parties under contingent claims (e.g. option) promise payments only if a specific event happens. The size of the derivatives market is difficult to determine because many transactions occur privately. Many analysts estimate it to be as big as 1.2 quadrillion USD, and this complex market has become an important part of investment.


The Purposes of Derivatives


In today's economic systems, derivative markets serve the following purposes:


  • Set future prices for the underlying assets with uncertain future values,
  • Assist risk management by locking in a price for a future transaction,
  • Improve market efficiency for underlying assets by reducing arbitrage opportunity, and
  • Provide a means to invest with a lower transaction cost.

Pricing Derivatives


A fundamental rule of derivatives pricing models is the law of one price, which states that no arbitrage opportunities should be available. In order to eliminate arbitrage, we equate the derivatives prices with the present value of the future cash flows discounted at the risk-free rates. A simple pricing model is binomial model, where we determine price based on two possible future prices and their chances of occurrence. A more complex model like Black-Scholes model helped us price options with a risk-free rate and implied volatility.


Though a value of derivatives should be equal to the present value of the future cash flows at the time of transaction, derivatives can be more or less valuable, depending on how the underlying assets perform. We will provide the tools that facilitate these pricing and valuation processes.
